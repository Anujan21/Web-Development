FUTURE EVENTS

The future is something that can't be predicted with absolute certainty. There are scientific theories that may accurately predict what might possibly occur in the future, but as with anything, events in the future can't be guaranteed. Regardless, scientists have unraveled a lot of truth of what's beyond the atmosphere of this planet, and continue to discover more and more celestial objects. Using what they have discovered, and using fields such as astrophysics, quantum physics and space physics, they can come up with theories on how things might behave in the near future, as well as distant future.

Here are notable events to see in the distant future (in years):

  • 1 - Launch of ESA Mars Rover and Russion surface platform.
  • 100 - Long-duration lunar eclipse of approximately 106.1 minutes.
  • 10,000 - The red supergiant star Antares will likely have exploded in a supernova. The explosion is expected to be easily visible in daylight.
  • 50,000 - Due to lunar tides decelerating the Earth's rotation, the need for a leap second every day would arrive.
  • 100,000 - Most of the constellations we see in the sky will become unrecognizable due to the motion of stars through the Milky way.
  • 200,000 - Constellations that used to be visible from Earth will no longer be recognizable.
  • 500,000 - Earth will likely have been hit by an asteroid of roughly 1 km in diameter, assuming it cannot be averted.
  • 1,000,000 - Betelgeuse, a star that can swallow our Sun 20 times, is 100,000 times brighter and is located about 640 light years from Earth can be reaching the end of its lifespan.
  • 6,800,000 - DNA from the 21st century will have completely decayed.
  • 7,200,000 - Mount Rushmore has eroded away.
  • 10,000,000 - Earth is being threatened by lethal levels of gamma radiation.
  • 100,000,000 - The rings of Saturn have disappeared.
  • 180 million - Due to the gradual slowing down of Earth's rotation, a day on Earth will be one hour longer than it is today.
  • 600 million - Tidal acceleration moves the Moon far enough from Earth that total solar eclipses are no longer possible.
  • 1 billion - Earth is becoming too hot to support liquid water.
  • 4 billion - The solar system will die when the Sun goes supernova.
  • 5 billion - Andromeda galaxy will collide with the Milky Way galaxy and form a larger galaxy called Milkdromeda Galaxy.
